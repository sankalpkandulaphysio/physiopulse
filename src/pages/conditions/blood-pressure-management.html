Exercise Therapy for Hypertension

Regular, structured exercise is one of the most effective non-pharmacological interventions for high blood pressure, capable of reducing systolic blood pressure by 5–8 mmHg — equivalent to first-line medication in many cases.

At PhysioPulse, our physiotherapists design individualised exercise programs for people with hypertension, guided by clinical evidence and delivered with appropriate monitoring. We also address the musculoskeletal effects of long-term hypertension and medication use, providing a holistic approach to your cardiovascular health.

Our Hypertension Physiotherapy Approach

❤️

Aerobic Exercise Prescription

Structured aerobic exercise — walking, cycling, and aquatic programs — delivered at the right intensity to achieve clinically meaningful blood pressure reduction.

💪

Resistance Training

Evidence-based resistance programs using moderate loads that improve arterial compliance and reduce peripheral resistance for sustained BP reduction.

😌

Stress Reduction Techniques

Breathing exercises, progressive relaxation, and mindfulness movement strategies that activate the parasympathetic nervous system to lower baseline blood pressure.

📈

Monitored Progression

Regular blood pressure checks, RPE monitoring, and load adjustment to ensure safe, effective progression throughout your program.

Who Benefits from this Program

🔴

Stage 1 Hypertension

Systolic 130–139 or diastolic 80–89 — lifestyle and exercise intervention can often avoid or delay medication.

💊

Medication Users

Exercise therapy reduces medication dependence and enhances the effects of antihypertensive drugs in those already on treatment.

⚡

Cardiovascular Risk Reduction

Those with multiple risk factors — diabetes, obesity, high cholesterol — benefit from combined exercise and lifestyle intervention.

🏃

Post-Cardiac Event

Supervised cardiac rehabilitation exercise following heart attack, bypass surgery, or heart failure.
