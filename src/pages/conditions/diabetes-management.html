Evidence-based exercise therapy and physiotherapy for Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes — improving blood glucose control, cardiovascular health, and preventing long-term complications.

Physiotherapy's Role in Diabetes Care

Exercise is recognised as a cornerstone of diabetes management, with the ability to improve insulin sensitivity, lower HbA1c, reduce cardiovascular risk, and enhance quality of life significantly.

At PhysioPulse, our physiotherapists design individualised, medically-informed exercise programs for people living with diabetes. We also treat the musculoskeletal complications of diabetes — including diabetic neuropathy, frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis is 5x more common in diabetics), and foot pain — providing comprehensive support for your health.

Our Diabetes Physiotherapy Programs

🏃

Structured Exercise Prescription

Individually designed aerobic and resistance exercise programs that effectively lower blood glucose and improve insulin sensitivity without hypoglycaemia risk.

⚡

Diabetic Neuropathy Management

Targeted therapy for peripheral neuropathy — balance training, nerve mobilisation, and sensory re-education to reduce fall risk and pain.

🦶

Foot & Lower Limb Care

Exercise and advice to improve foot circulation, prevent ulcers, and manage diabetic foot complications in coordination with your medical team.

📊

Progress Monitoring

Regular reassessment of strength, functional capacity, and glycaemic response to exercise to ensure your program evolves appropriately.

How Physiotherapy Helps at Every Stage

🔴

Newly Diagnosed

Starting a safe, effective exercise program early is one of the most powerful interventions for Type 2 diabetes — we guide you from day one.

⚡

Numbness & Neuropathy

Peripheral neuropathy causing tingling, numbness, or pain in the feet and hands — physiotherapy can significantly reduce symptoms.

🏋️

Weight Management

Exercise therapy as a key component of weight loss programs that improve blood glucose control and reduce medication dependence.

🦵

Musculoskeletal Complications

Diabetes significantly increases risk of frozen shoulder, carpal tunnel, and tendinopathy — all treatable with physiotherapy.
