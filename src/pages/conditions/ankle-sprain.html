Understanding Ankle Sprains

Ankle sprains are among the most common sports and activity-related injuries. While often dismissed as minor, improperly treated ankle sprains can lead to chronic instability, recurring injuries, and long-term problems. Physiotherapy is essential for complete healing and prevention of future ankle issues.

Our comprehensive ankle rehabilitation program focuses on restoring strength, stability, balance, and proprioception. We help you recover fully from your ankle sprain and return to your activities with confidence, while reducing the risk of re-injury.

Common Ankle Injuries We Treat

Ankle problems range from acute sprains to chronic instability requiring specialized care.

Lateral Ankle Sprain

Most common type involving the outside ligaments of the ankle from rolling the foot inward during walking, running, or sports.

High Ankle Sprain

Injury to the ligaments connecting the tibia and fibula, typically from twisting or rotation, requiring longer recovery time.

Chronic Instability

Persistent feeling of ankle giving way from previous sprains that didn't heal properly, leading to recurrent injuries.

Achilles Tendinitis

Inflammation and pain in the Achilles tendon from overuse, poor footwear, or training errors affecting running and jumping.

Peroneal Tendinitis

Inflammation of tendons on the outside of the ankle causing lateral ankle pain and weakness in foot eversion.

Fracture Recovery

Rehabilitation after ankle fractures to restore mobility, strength, and function following immobilization or surgery.

How Physiotherapy Helps Ankle Sprains

Our proven rehabilitation protocol ensures complete healing and prevents chronic problems.

1

Initial Assessment

Thorough evaluation of injury severity, swelling, range of motion, strength, and stability to determine the appropriate treatment approach.

2

Reduce Pain & Swelling

Manual therapy, gentle mobilization, and therapeutic techniques to manage pain, reduce swelling, and restore initial mobility.

3

Strengthen & Stabilize

Progressive strengthening exercises for ankle muscles, balance training, and proprioception work to restore stability and prevent re-injury.

4

Return to Activity

Sport-specific training, agility drills, and functional exercises to safely return you to running, sports, and demanding activities.

When to Seek Care

🔴

Immediate Injury

Significant pain, swelling, or inability to bear weight on your ankle immediately after rolling or twisting it.

💧

Persistent Swelling

Ankle swelling lasting more than a few days or bruising developing around the ankle and foot area.

🔒

Instability

Feeling like your ankle is unstable, weak, or giving way during walking or when changing direction.

🔄

Recurring Sprains

History of multiple ankle sprains or feeling like you keep "re-injuring" the same ankle repeatedly.

⚾

Sports Return

Need to safely return to sports like football, basketball, tennis, or running after an ankle injury.

🚶

Walking Difficulty

Persistent pain or difficulty walking normally several days after an ankle injury, despite rest and ice.
