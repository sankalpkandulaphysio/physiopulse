What is Workplace Health?

Workplace health services focus on preventing and treating work-related injuries and conditions, particularly those affecting office workers and IT professionals. Our programs combine ergonomic assessments, postural correction, and targeted treatment to address the unique challenges of modern desk-based work.

From carpal tunnel syndrome to chronic neck pain, we understand the physical demands of long hours at a computer. Our workplace health solutions help you work comfortably, prevent injuries, and maintain productivity without compromising your physical wellbeing.

Common Workplace Conditions We Treat

Expert care for the most common office and desk-related health issues.

Neck & Shoulder Pain

Tech neck, upper crossed syndrome, shoulder tension, trapezius strain from poor monitor position and prolonged sitting.

Back Pain

Lower back pain from sitting, lumbar strain, poor chair ergonomics, and inadequate postural support.

Repetitive Strain Injury

Carpal tunnel syndrome, mouse shoulder, tennis elbow from keyboard and mouse use, typing-related wrist pain.

Headaches

Tension headaches, eye strain headaches, and cervicogenic headaches from poor posture and screen time.

Postural Issues

Forward head posture, rounded shoulders, slouching, and muscular imbalances from prolonged sitting.

Wrist & Hand Pain

Wrist strain, finger stiffness, thumb pain, and hand fatigue from computer work and phone use.

Our Workplace Health Approach

Comprehensive solutions to create a healthier, more comfortable work environment.

1

Ergonomic Assessment

Detailed evaluation of your workstation setup, posture, equipment positioning, and work habits to identify risk factors and problem areas.

2

Workspace Optimization

Provide specific recommendations for desk height, chair adjustment, monitor position, keyboard placement, and lighting to create an ergonomic setup.

3

Treatment & Correction

Targeted physiotherapy treatment for existing pain and dysfunction, combined with exercises to correct postural imbalances and strengthen weak areas.

4

Prevention Strategies

Education on proper posture, regular movement breaks, stretching routines, and work habits to prevent future injuries and maintain comfort.
