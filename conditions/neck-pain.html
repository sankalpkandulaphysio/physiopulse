Understanding Neck Pain

Neck pain affects millions of people, especially those who spend long hours at computers or using mobile devices. Whether you're dealing with acute neck pain from a recent injury or chronic stiffness that has developed over time, physiotherapy offers effective solutions to restore comfort, mobility, and function.

Our comprehensive neck pain treatment addresses the underlying causes, including muscle tension, poor posture, joint dysfunction, and nerve irritation. Through hands-on therapy, corrective exercises, and ergonomic education, we help you achieve lasting relief and prevent future episodes.

Common Causes of Neck Pain

Neck pain can stem from various factors affecting the cervical spine, muscles, and nerves.

Tech Neck

Forward head posture from prolonged phone and computer use, creating strain on neck muscles and cervical spine alignment.

Muscle Strain

Overuse, poor sleeping position, stress-related tension, or sudden movements causing neck muscle spasm and pain.

Whiplash Injury

Sudden forceful back-and-forth movement of the neck from car accidents or sports impacts, damaging soft tissues.

Cervical Disc Issues

Herniated or degenerative discs in the neck causing pain, stiffness, and potential nerve compression symptoms.

Pinched Nerve

Nerve root compression causing sharp pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness radiating into the shoulder, arm, or hand.

Arthritis

Cervical spondylosis, osteoarthritis, or age-related wear affecting neck joints and causing stiffness and pain.

How Physiotherapy Helps Neck Pain

Our specialized treatment combines proven techniques to relieve pain and restore neck function.

1

Detailed Assessment

Thorough evaluation of neck range of motion, posture, muscle strength, joint mobility, and nerve function to pinpoint the exact cause of your pain.

2

Manual Therapy

Gentle cervical mobilization, soft tissue massage, myofascial release, and trigger point therapy to reduce muscle tension and restore joint mobility.

3

Exercise Prescription

Targeted neck strengthening, stretching, and postural exercises to correct muscle imbalances, improve alignment, and prevent recurrence.

4

Ergonomic Guidance

Education on proper workstation setup, sleeping positions, phone use, and daily habits to minimize neck strain and maintain improvements.

When to Seek Care

🔴

Persistent Stiffness

Neck pain or stiffness lasting more than a few days, making it difficult to turn your head or look over your shoulder.

⚡

Radiating Symptoms

Pain, numbness, or tingling spreading from your neck into your shoulders, arms, hands, or fingers.

🤕

Headaches

Frequent headaches starting from the base of your skull or tension headaches associated with neck tightness.

💼

Work Impact

Neck pain interfering with your ability to work at a computer, drive, or perform daily activities comfortably.

🔄

Recurring Pain

Experiencing repeated episodes of neck pain or stiffness that keeps returning despite temporary relief.

🚗

After Injury

Neck pain following a car accident, fall, sports injury, or any trauma to the head or neck region.
