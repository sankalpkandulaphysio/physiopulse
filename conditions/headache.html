Understanding Headaches

Many headaches originate from neck dysfunction, muscle tension, and poor posture—conditions that physiotherapy can effectively address. If you suffer from frequent headaches, especially those that start at the base of your skull or accompany neck pain and stiffness, physiotherapy may provide the lasting relief you've been seeking.

Our specialized headache treatment program targets the musculoskeletal causes of your headaches through manual therapy, postural correction, and therapeutic exercises. We help you reduce headache frequency, intensity, and duration without relying solely on pain medications.

Types of Headaches We Treat

Physiotherapy is highly effective for headaches with musculoskeletal origins.

Tension Headaches

Most common type caused by muscle tension in the neck, shoulders, and scalp, often from stress or prolonged poor posture.

Cervicogenic Headaches

Headaches originating from neck problems, joints, or muscles, typically felt on one side starting from the base of the skull.

Postural Headaches

Headaches triggered by poor desk posture, forward head position, or prolonged computer work causing neck strain.

Migraine with Neck Component

Migraines accompanied by neck pain or triggered by neck dysfunction, benefiting from physiotherapy alongside medical management.

Jaw-Related Headaches

TMJ dysfunction and jaw clenching causing temple and forehead pain, often related to stress and posture.

Post-Whiplash Headaches

Persistent headaches following car accidents or neck injuries, requiring specialized neck treatment and rehabilitation.

How Physiotherapy Helps Headaches

Our targeted approach addresses the root causes of musculoskeletal headaches.

1

Comprehensive Assessment

Detailed evaluation of neck mobility, posture, muscle tension, trigger points, and jaw function to identify headache sources.

2

Manual Therapy

Gentle neck mobilization, soft tissue massage, trigger point release, and myofascial techniques to reduce muscle tension and restore joint function.

3

Postural Correction

Exercises to strengthen neck muscles, improve posture, and correct forward head position that contributes to headaches.

4

Prevention Strategies

Education on ergonomics, stress management techniques, stretching routines, and lifestyle modifications to prevent headache recurrence.

When to Seek Care

🔴

Frequent Episodes

Experiencing headaches more than twice a week or headaches that are becoming more frequent over time.

💼

Work-Related

Headaches that worsen during or after work, especially if you work at a desk or computer for extended periods.

🔙

Neck Association

Headaches accompanied by neck pain, stiffness, or headaches that start from the base of your skull.

💊

Medication Overuse

Relying on pain medications frequently to manage headaches without addressing the underlying cause.

📍

One-Sided Pain

Consistent headaches on one side of your head or pain that always starts in the same location.

🎯

Pattern Recognition

Headaches triggered by specific positions, activities, or times of day, suggesting mechanical causes.
