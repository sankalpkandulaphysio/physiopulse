Understanding Hip Pain

Hip pain can significantly impact your ability to walk, climb stairs, sit comfortably, and perform daily activities. Whether you're experiencing pain from arthritis, a sports injury, or recovering from hip surgery, physiotherapy plays a crucial role in restoring hip function, reducing pain, and improving your quality of life.

Our comprehensive hip rehabilitation program addresses all aspects of hip health, including joint mobility, muscle strength, gait mechanics, and functional movement. Through evidence-based treatment, we help you regain pain-free movement and return to the activities you enjoy.

Common Causes of Hip Pain

Hip pain can result from various conditions affecting the joint, muscles, and surrounding structures.

Hip Arthritis

Osteoarthritis causing hip joint degeneration, stiffness, pain with walking, and reduced range of motion.

Hip Bursitis

Inflammation of the fluid-filled sacs cushioning the hip, causing lateral hip pain, especially when lying on that side.

Labral Tears

Damage to the cartilage rim of the hip socket causing deep hip pain, clicking, and catching sensations.

IT Band Syndrome

Tightness or inflammation of the iliotibial band causing lateral hip and thigh pain, common in runners.

Hip Replacement Recovery

Rehabilitation after total hip replacement surgery to restore strength, mobility, and functional independence.

Muscle Strains

Hip flexor, groin, or gluteal muscle strains from sports, overuse, or sudden movements causing pain and weakness.

How Physiotherapy Helps Hip Pain

Our targeted approach addresses hip dysfunction through comprehensive rehabilitation.

1

Detailed Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of hip range of motion, strength, gait analysis, muscle imbalances, and functional limitations.

2

Manual Therapy

Joint mobilization, soft tissue massage, myofascial release, and stretching to reduce pain, improve mobility, and restore joint mechanics.

3

Strengthening Program

Progressive hip, gluteal, and core strengthening exercises to improve stability, reduce joint stress, and enhance function.

4

Functional Training

Gait retraining, balance exercises, and activity-specific training to safely return you to walking, sports, and daily activities.

When to Seek Care

🔴

Persistent Pain

Hip or groin pain lasting more than a few days, especially if it worsens with activity or weight-bearing.

🚶

Walking Difficulty

Limping, difficulty walking, or needing to use a handrail when climbing stairs due to hip pain or weakness.

🔒

Stiffness

Significant hip stiffness, especially in the morning or after sitting, limiting your ability to bend or move your hip.

😴

Night Pain

Hip pain interfering with sleep, especially pain when lying on your side or difficulty finding comfortable positions.

🏥

Post-Surgery

Recovery after hip replacement, labral repair, or other hip surgery requiring professional rehabilitation.

📉

Reduced Activity

Giving up activities you enjoy like walking, running, dancing, or sports due to hip pain or limitations.
