Understanding Acidity & Its Physiotherapy Connection

Acidity and acid reflux are often worsened by poor posture, diaphragmatic dysfunction, and stress-related muscle tension — all areas where physiotherapy can make a significant difference.

At PhysioPulse, we work alongside your medical team to address the musculoskeletal and postural contributors to digestive issues, helping you achieve better symptom control and quality of life.

Our Approach to Acidity Management

🧘

Postural Correction

Forward-head and slumped postures compress the stomach and worsen reflux. We correct spinal alignment to relieve abdominal pressure.

🌬️

Diaphragmatic Breathing

Training the diaphragm to function optimally improves the lower oesophageal sphincter and reduces reflux episodes significantly.

🤲

Visceral Manual Therapy

Gentle manual therapy techniques targeting the abdomen and thoracic spine can reduce tension around digestive organs.

😌

Stress & Tension Management

Chronic stress is a major trigger for acidity. We teach relaxation techniques, breathing exercises, and body awareness to reduce sympathetic overload.

When Physiotherapy Helps Acidity

🔴

Persistent Reflux

Acid reflux that recurs despite medication — physiotherapy addresses the postural and functional contributors.

⚡

Postural Acidity

Worsening of symptoms during prolonged sitting, desk work, or after meals in a slumped position.

🫁

Breathing Dysfunction

Shallow chest breathing or diaphragm weakness contributing to reduced lower oesophageal sphincter tone.

💼

Work-Related Symptoms

Desk workers who experience worsening reflux throughout the workday due to ergonomic factors.
