Understanding Back Pain

Back pain is one of the most common reasons people seek physiotherapy treatment. Whether you're experiencing acute lower back pain from a sudden injury or chronic pain that has persisted for months or years, physiotherapy offers effective, evidence-based solutions to help you recover and prevent future episodes.

Our comprehensive approach addresses the root cause of your back pain, not just the symptoms. Through detailed assessment, manual therapy, targeted exercises, and patient education, we help you return to pain-free movement and activities you enjoy.

Common Causes of Back Pain

Back pain can result from various factors affecting the spine, muscles, and surrounding structures.

Muscle Strain

Overuse, poor lifting technique, sudden movements, or prolonged sitting causing muscle and ligament strain in the back.

Herniated Disc

Disc material pressing on spinal nerves, causing pain, numbness, or weakness radiating down the leg (sciatica).

Poor Posture

Prolonged sitting, desk work, slouching, and forward head posture creating chronic strain on back muscles and spine.

Degenerative Changes

Age-related wear and tear, arthritis, spinal stenosis, and disc degeneration affecting spinal structures.

Spinal Misalignment

Scoliosis, vertebral dysfunction, or sacroiliac joint problems affecting spinal mechanics and causing pain.

Injury or Trauma

Sports injuries, falls, car accidents, or workplace incidents causing acute back pain and dysfunction.

How Physiotherapy Helps Back Pain

Our evidence-based approach combines multiple treatment strategies for comprehensive back pain relief.

1

Comprehensive Assessment

Detailed evaluation of your spine, posture, movement patterns, strength, and flexibility to identify the exact cause of your back pain and contributing factors.

2

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques including spinal mobilization, soft tissue massage, myofascial release, and joint manipulation to reduce pain and restore mobility.

3

Therapeutic Exercise

Customized exercise program to strengthen core muscles, improve flexibility, correct postural imbalances, and stabilize the spine for long-term relief.

4

Education & Prevention

Teaching proper body mechanics, ergonomic principles, lifting techniques, and self-management strategies to prevent recurrence and maintain back health.

When to Seek Care

🔴

Persistent Pain

Back pain lasting more than a few days or pain that doesn't improve with rest and home care measures.

⚡

Radiating Pain

Pain traveling down your leg, buttock, or thigh, often accompanied by numbness, tingling, or weakness.

🚫

Limited Mobility

Difficulty bending, standing up straight, walking, or performing daily activities due to back pain or stiffness.

💊

Medication Dependence

Relying on pain medications regularly to manage your back pain without addressing the underlying cause.

🔄

Recurring Episodes

Experiencing repeated episodes of back pain that keep coming back after temporary relief.

😴

Sleep Disruption

Back pain interfering with your sleep, making it difficult to find a comfortable sleeping position.
